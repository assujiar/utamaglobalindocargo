# Single Source of Truth (SSOT) – Business & Brand Fundamentals

This document captures all immutable business truths and brand details that must
be preserved in the future rebuild.  These items form the non‑negotiable core
of UGC’s identity and service offerings.

## Brand Identity

| Element | Description |
| --- | --- |

  name must appear consistently across the site, metadata, schema markup and

### Relationship between tagline and hero headline

* The tagline **“We Care What We Deliver”** functions as a **brand promise** and
  should be anchored in the brand signature (e.g. footer, about page, or
  within a mission statement).  It should not be repurposed as a primary hero
  headline or diluted into descriptive copy.  It communicates the company’s
  care, accountability and human values.
* The hero headline **“One line of control across every handoff.”** is the
  **opening narrative** that introduces the site’s value proposition.  It
  conveys operational control, integration and reliability.  The hero should
  support this narrative with a short sub‑headline explaining the range of
  services and the benefit of dealing with one accountable partner.
* Do not collapse the tagline and hero into one line, and do not substitute
  the tagline for the hero headline.  They serve complementary but distinct
  roles.

## Business Basics

- **Headquarters**: Jakarta, Indonesia.  UGC is an Indonesian freight
  forwarder and logistics company serving domestic and international routes.
- **Industries served**: manufacturing, commodities, FMCG, e‑commerce,
  pharmaceutical, energy and other sectors requiring reliable logistics.  These
  industries are mentioned in the About page and should be reflected in
  future content.
- **Core promise**: one point of coordination across the entire supply chain.
  UGC manages transport mode selection, documentation, customs clearance and
  warehousing so clients avoid juggling multiple vendors.
- **Brand tone**: honest, operator‑grade, no empty claims.  Content should
  speak to executives looking for reliability rather than generic “premium”
  language.

## Content Facts to Preserve

* **Local operations** – UGC coordinates domestic distribution across Indonesia,
  including remote islands.  This operational detail must remain, even if the
  design changes.
* **International reach** – The company arranges export and import via ocean and
  air to and from multiple countries.  Do not constrain the rebuild to
  “regional” when the business engages globally.
* **One point of contact** – The promise of a single point of coordination
  (“satu titik koordinasi”) is fundamental.  It should remain a narrative
  pillar and be echoed throughout the site.
* **Client industries** – Manufacturing, commodities, FMCG, e‑commerce,
  pharmaceutical and energy are core industries served.  Future content may
  expand on industry pages but should not invent markets the company does not
  serve.
* **Honest numbers** – Any operational statistics (e.g. number of countries
  served, containers per month) must be based on verifiable internal data and
  use approximate language where appropriate (e.g. “sekitar 40 %” instead of
  “40 %” precisely).  If numbers cannot be verified, do not publish them.
